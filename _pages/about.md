I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Clarkson University under the supervision of Dr. Cetin Cetinkaya.

My research focuses on ultrasonic wave propagation, nondestructive evaluation, viscoelastic material characterization, and pharmaceutical oral solid dosage systems.

## Research Interests

- Ultrasonic wave propagation
- Acoustic material characterization
- Pharmaceutical tablet mechanics
- Viscoelastic and microstructural modeling
- Nondestructive evaluation (NDE)
- Signal processing and spectral analysis
- Machine learning applications in engineering

## Current Research

My current work investigates the effects of moisture and thermal exposure on compressed oral solid dosage forms using ultrasonic techniques. The study integrates experimental ultrasonic measurements, frequency-domain analysis, and inverse viscoelastic modeling to characterize irreversible microstructural changes in pharmaceutical tablets.
